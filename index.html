Exploring SPIN

Sparsifying and Integrating Internal Neurons in Large Language Models for Text Classification

Difan Jiao, Yilun Liu, Zhenwei Tang, Daniel Matter, Jürgen Pfeffer, Ashton Anderson

At the beginning...

For each given sentence, we retrieve its neuron activation patterns of FFN neurons at each layer of the transformer model.

Pooling Operation

A max pooling is then performed across all tokens, yielding each neuron's maximum activation record for the sentence.

Layer-Wise Linear Probes

Linear probing is applied layer-wise for identifying the salient neurons for the targeted task in each layer of the model.

The magnitude of the learned weights in the L1-regularized logistic regressioncan are interpreted as indicators of the relative importance or contribution to the prediction, i.e., saliency, of each neuron.

Salient Neuron Selection

The selection of salient neurons is guided by a sparsification threshold η, which serves as a metric for determining the cumulative contribution of the most significant neurons. We select neurons with largest weights, until their cumulative summation reaches η.

The internal neurons are then sparsified based on their saliency.

Salient Neuron Integration

Following the sparsification of internal neurons from each respective layer, we proceed to integrate them as cross-layer multi-grained representations into the classification head.

Classification Head

For each layer, the integrated neurons from that layer and below are concatenated and feed into the classification head.
